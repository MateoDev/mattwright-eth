I've been building in the shadows for too long.

For the past few years, I've been heads-down on **EVM Systems** and **Gaia**, working on what I believe is the future of autonomous agent infrastructure. We're building systems that let AI agents operate with real economic power — coordinating work, managing capital, and making decisions that matter.

But I realized something recently: **the best builders share their journey**.

## Why Now?

The agent economy is happening faster than anyone expected. We're not talking about chatbots or assistants anymore. We're talking about autonomous entities that can:

- Execute complex workflows across multiple systems
- Coordinate with other agents to complete tasks
- Manage their own wallets and make economic decisions
- Learn and adapt from their experiences

This isn't science fiction. It's happening right now, and the infrastructure layer is wide open.

## What I'm Building

**EVM Systems** is my attempt to solve the infrastructure problem for autonomous agents. We're building:

- **Agent coordination protocols** — how agents discover, trust, and work with each other
- **Economic primitives** — wallet management, payment rails, and value transfer for agents
- **Memory and context systems** — giving agents the ability to remember and build on past experiences
- **Deployment infrastructure** — making it dead simple to launch and manage agent workforces

The vision is simple: make it as easy to deploy an agent workforce as it is to spin up a cloud server.

## The SOUL.md Approach

One thing I've learned: **agents need personality and purpose**. Not in a gimmicky way, but in a structural way.

That's why we use `SOUL.md` and `AGENTS.md` files to define the core identity and behavioral patterns of our agent systems. It's like giving your codebase a constitution — a set of immutable principles that guide every decision.

This isn't just philosophical. It's practical. When you're running dozens of autonomous agents, you need a way to ensure they're all aligned with your mission. Text-based identity files make that possible.

## What to Expect Here

I'm committing to writing regularly about:

- Building agentic infrastructure from scratch
- Lessons learned running autonomous agent systems in production
- The economics of AI-native work
- Web3 + AI convergence (it's happening whether crypto wants it or not)
- Technical deep-dives on agent coordination and memory systems

This blog is a receipt of the work. Raw, unfiltered, and probably a little too technical at times.

If you're building in the agent space, or just curious about where this is all heading, follow along. I promise it'll be interesting.

I'm here for it. Let's build something that matters.
